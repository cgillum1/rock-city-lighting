Page Layout — How should the sections flow?

Three structures for a scroll-down landing page. All include the same sections, just in different order and emphasis.

🖼 HERO — Full screen photo + tagline + "Get a Free Quote" button
👤 ABOUT — 2 sentences about Rock City Lighting
🔧 SERVICES — Residential / Landscape cards
📸 GALLERY — Photo grid of your best work
⭐ TESTIMONIALS — (placeholder for future reviews)
📋 CONTACT FORM — Get a free quote
— FOOTER —

A — Classic Flow

Introduce → explain services → show work → convert. The most familiar structure. Visitors always know where they are. Best if you want a "safe" professional feel.

🖼 HERO — Full screen photo + tagline + "See Our Work" button
📸 GALLERY — Big, dominant photo showcase right up top
🔧 SERVICES — What you offer
👤 ABOUT — Who you are
⭐ TESTIMONIALS
📋 CONTACT FORM
— FOOTER —

B — Portfolio-First ⭐ Recommended

Let the photos do the talking immediately. Hero gets them hooked, then the gallery closes the deal before they've even read a word. Best for visual trades like lighting.

🖼 HERO — Full screen photo + bold statement
💡 WHY LIGHTING — "Transform your home at night" hook section
📸 GALLERY — Split: before/after or project spotlight cards
🔧 SERVICES
👤 ABOUT
📋 CONTACT FORM
— FOOTER —

C — Story-Driven

Sells the dream before showing the work. A short "why outdoor lighting matters" section primes visitors emotionally before they see your portfolio. More persuasive but longer.

Platform Approach — Which fits Rock City Lighting?

Three real options. Pick the one that feels right — we'll discuss trade-offs after.

A

WordPress ⭐ Recommended

The world's most popular CMS. You get a full admin panel to add project photos, write posts, manage everything — no code. Thousands of plugins including social media feeds when you're ready. Hosts on your existing Namecheap account.

Pros

  • Full control, you own everything
  • Free software
  • Best plugin ecosystem (galleries, social feeds, SEO)
  • Works on Namecheap
  • Huge community if you ever get stuck

Cons

  • Slightly more setup upfront
  • Needs occasional maintenance (updates)

Cost ~$5–10/mo hosting on Namecheap

B

Webflow

A premium website builder with a visual editor and built-in CMS for projects/posts. Stunning design output. You manage everything through a clean dashboard. Social embeds available via copy-paste codes.

Pros

  • Beautiful design, fast and secure
  • Clean CMS editor
  • No maintenance needed

Cons

  • $23–39/mo ongoing cost
  • Less flexible than WordPress
  • Steeper learning curve for editor

Cost $23–39/mo

C

Squarespace / Wix

The easiest all-in-one option. Drag-and-drop, built-in social feed blocks, portfolio templates. Ideal if you want the absolute lowest friction to get something live fast.

Pros

  • Easiest to use
  • Social integrations built in
  • Templates for portfolios

Cons

  • Least flexible — hard to customize deeply
  • You're renting, not owning
  • Harder to stand out from other sites

Cost $16–25/mo
